About the GRE

The Graduate Record Examinations is a standardized test that is an admissions requirement for many graduate schools in the United States and Canada, as well as a few other countries. The GRE is owned and administered by Educational Testing Service.

One Test. More Opportunities for Success.

Whether you are planning to go to graduate, business, or law school or just exploring your options, you are taking an important step toward your future. It is a smart move to show schools your best with the GRE General Test.

We are here to support you with resources that help you prepare for the test, connect with recruiters, build a competitive application and more.

Organization of session-II mocks- UPDATED (Posted on 17-SEP-2023)

The paper pattern had been released by the ETS on 15th September 2023. There are a few amendments to the same. The number of questions per section as per the given combination could vary with mocks as well as the ongoing mock.

Tie breaker guidelines (Posted on 10-SEP-2023)

The following tie breaker guidelines have been suggested:

  1. The initial subject to tie breaker is the score achieved by the candidate in quantitative reasoning
  2. The next subject to tie breaker is the score achieved by the candidate in verbal reasoning
  3. If the scores are still the same, then the scores achieved by the candidate in the AWA section would be considered
  4. If for some reason the scores still match, then the date on which the candidate registered would be considered as tie breaker. The candidate who would have registered earlier would stand out in terms of the same
  5. If the tie breaker still exists, the same position would be given to all the candidates involved.

**This tie breaker exists in case there are multiple candidates for one seat.
